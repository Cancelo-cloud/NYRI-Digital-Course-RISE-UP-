    ðŸ§© Applying Systems Thinking: Real-Life Case Studies

    DUCT Version:

    1. Leaking Tap at a Public Facility
    Conventional Thinking:
    "The municipality is to blame."
    Systems Thinking:
    "What prevented early action? Could a local resident have reported it sooner or attempted a basic fix? How might we build a system for faster community response?"
    2. Children Keep Skipping the After-School Learning Program
    Conventional Thinking:
    "The kids are not serious about learning."
    Systems Thinking:
    "Are the sessions engaging? Are their basic needs (like food or safety) being met first? What do the children actually want? Are parents and teachers involved?"
    3. Litter Everywhere After Weekend Events
    Conventional Thinking:
    "People are dirty and careless."
    Systems Thinking:
    "Were there enough bins or clean-up crews? Could incentives or reminders help? Was there any local involvement in the event planning?"
    4. Missed Deadlines on a Team Project
    Conventional Thinking:
    "Someone on the team isn't pulling their weight."
    Systems Thinking:
    "Are the roles clear? Were expectations realistic? Is communication strong enough? Did we rely too heavily on one person?"
    5. High Dropout Rate in a Skills Training Program
    Conventional Thinking:
    "People don't want to improve their lives."
    Systems Thinking:
    "Were transport costs an issue? Were the sessions at the right time? Were participants supported and followed up with? Is the training aligned with their actual goals?"
